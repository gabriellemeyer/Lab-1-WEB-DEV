Penguins are remarkable seabirds that thrive in some of the coldest environments on Earth, using dense feathers, layers of fat, and social behavior to survive extreme conditions. They cannot fly, yet they swim with incredible speed and agility, hunting fish, squid, and krill beneath icy waters. Their strong flippers, streamlined bodies, and excellent vision make them perfectly adapted for life in the ocean during long seasonal migrations across vast southern seas with remarkable endurance skills.
